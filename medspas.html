The Problem

What you can't see from the owner's chair

Your marketing is fine. Your providers are booked. Your reviews are strong. And yet the P&L isn't matching what the top of the funnel should be producing. That's not a demand problem — it's almost always operational leakage, hiding in four specific places.

01

Missed inbound calls

20–30% of new-patient calls go unanswered on the first try. A missed call converts to a missed booking about 60% of the time — and a missed booking is a missed treatment plan, not just a missed visit.

02

Consult follow-up that dies in 72 hours

Most prospects who cool off after a consult do so within three days. Your front desk is in treatment. Your CRM doesn't know the difference between a warm lead and a cold one. Revenue evaporates quietly.

03

No-shows and late cancels

A 15–25% no-show rate is normal. A 5–8% rate is achievable. The difference is 10–15 points of margin, every month, compounding, with no new marketing spend.

04

Rebooking and reactivation

40% of first-time med spa clients never return. You feel it, and nobody on your team owns it. The LTV gap between a 40% and a 60% return rate is usually 2–3x the entire marketing budget.

These are not tool problems. They are architecture problems. Buying more software doesn't fix them — it makes the leaks faster.
